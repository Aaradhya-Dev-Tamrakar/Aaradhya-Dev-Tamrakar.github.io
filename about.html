I'm a fourth-year Electronics, Communication & Information Engineering student at Kathmandu Engineering College, IOE, Tribhuvan University — working at the intersection of embedded systems, applied machine learning, and the kind of robotics that actually has to run outdoors.

I'm currently a Fuse AI Fellow (Fusemachines, 2026 cohort), having built an agentic Text-to-SQL pipeline, tree-based ensemble models with SHAP explainability, Bayesian inference workflows in PyMC, and a full customer-segmentation pipeline (K-Means/Hierarchical/DBSCAN) on the UCI Online Retail II dataset. In parallel, I'm an NSSR DataCamp Fellow (Cohort 2), progressing through SQL Associate and Python Data Associate certification tracks after completing six applied GenAI projects in Phase 1.

My BEI major project, PrakopNet, is a solar-powered LoRa mesh multi-hazard early-warning platform for remote Nepal — ESP32 sensor nodes, federated TFLite edge inference, and LSTM-based hazard-score fusion at a Raspberry Pi 4B gateway. Beyond the bench, I serve as Vice Chair of the IEEE KEC KTM Student Branch and Event Manager at the Electronics Project Club, and volunteer as a Makerspace Ambassador running the club's web infrastructure.
