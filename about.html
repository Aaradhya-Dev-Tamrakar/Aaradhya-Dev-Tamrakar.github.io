I'm a fourth-year Electronics, Communication & Information Engineering student at Kathmandu Engineering College, IOE, Tribhuvan University — working at the intersection of embedded systems, applied machine learning, and the kind of robotics that actually has to run outdoors.

I'm a Fuse AI Fellow (Fusemachines, 2026 cohort), having built an agentic Text-to-SQL pipeline, tree-based ensemble models with SHAP explainability, Bayesian inference workflows in PyMC, and a full customer-segmentation pipeline (K-Means/Hierarchical/DBSCAN) on the UCI Online Retail II dataset. My capstone is a vision-model fairness & bias audit tool — a diagnostic layer that flags demographic performance disparities in deployed classifiers without retraining them — built with fellow Fellow Tisha Manandhar. In parallel, I'm an NSSR DataCamp Fellow (Cohort 2), progressing through SQL Associate and Python Data Associate certification tracks after completing six applied GenAI projects in Phase 1.

My BEI major project, SPARK, is a two-layer, fully on-device fall-detection wearable with an explainable gateway pipeline for eldercare — an MPU6050 IMU feeding a threshold gate and a TFLite Micro CNN on-device, with SHAP-attributed alerts and clinical-format incident reports at a Raspberry Pi 4B gateway. Beyond the bench, I serve as Vice Chair of the IEEE KEC KTM Student Branch and Event Manager at the Electronics Project Club, and volunteer as a Makerspace Ambassador running the club's web infrastructure.
